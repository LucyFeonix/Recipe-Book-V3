# Ingredients
## Marinade
- 3 cups [[Burgundy Wine|Burgundy wine]]
- 2 [[Yellow Onion|onions]], thinly sliced
- 2 [[Carrot|carrots]], chopped
- 2 tbsp [[Brandy|brandy]]
- 1 clove [[Garlic|garlic]], crushed
- 10 whole [[Black Pepper|black peppercorns]]
- 1 tsp [[Salt|salt]]
- 1 sprig [[Parsley|parsley]]
- 1 [[Bay Leaves|bay leaf]]
- 2 lbs [[Chuck Roast|chuck roast]], cubed
## Bourguignon
- 4 tbsp [[Olive Oil|olive oil]]
- ¼ lb [[Bacon|bacon]]
- 2 [[Yellow Onion|onions]], chopped
- 3 tbsp [[Flour|all-purpose flour]]
- 2 cloves [[Garlic|garlic]], crushed
- 1 tbsp [[Tomato Paste|tomato paste]]
- 10.5 oz [[Beef Broth|beef broth]]
- [[Salt|Salt]] and [[Black Pepper|pepper]] to taste
- 4 tbsp [[Unsalted Butter|unsalted butter]]
- 1 lb [[Button Mushrooms|mushrooms]], sliced
# Notes
- 8 servings
- Requirements:
- Time: 2 days, 3 hours, 30 minutes
# Directions
1. Mix the wine, sliced onions, carrots, brandy, garlic, peppercorns, salt, parsley, and bay leaf in a large bowl. Add beef and toss to coat evenly.
2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and marinate in the refrigerator for 2 days.
3. Preheat the oven to 300°F/150°C.
4. Strain beef from the marinade and shake off the excess, pat dry with paper towels. Strain the vegetables and set aside, and reserve the marinade. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches; cook until browned on all sides. Transfer the browned beef into a spearate medium bowl, and set aside.
5. Add bacon to the same skillet; cook and stir until lightly browned. Transfer bacon to the bowl with the browned beef. Drain the skillet and return it to the heat.  Pour 1 cup of reserved marinade into the skillet; bring to a boil while scraping the browned bits off the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. Return this to the reserved marinade.
6. Heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil in the same skillet. Add the chopped onions; cook and stir until just tender. Stir in the reserved onions and carrots from the marinade; mix well, then use a slotted spoon to transfer vegetables into the bowl with beef.
7. Return the skillet to the heat; stir flour in pan drippings until browned. Stir in the garlic and tomato paste until combined. Add the beef broth, remaining reserved marinade, salt,  and pepper. Bring to a boil, whisking to remove any flour lumps, pour over beef mixture in the bowl. Transfer beef mixture into a casserole or baking dish.
8. Cook in the preheated oven until the beef is tender, ±3 hours; stir occasionally and add water if needed.
9. About 10 minutes before serving, melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and sauté until lightly browned, 5-7 minutes; stir mushrooms into beef mixture until well combined.
