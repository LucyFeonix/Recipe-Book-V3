# Ingredients
- 4 12oz whole [[Trout|trout]], gutted
- 2 tsp [[Old Bay Seasoning|Old Bay seasoning]]
- 2 large slices multigrain [[Bread|bread]], crusts trimmed, torn into small pieces
- 3 tbsp [[Unsalted Butter|unsalted butter]]
- ¼ cup [[Shallots|shallots]], minced
- ¾ cup [[Bell Pepper|red bell pepper]], finely chopped
- 2 tsp [[Garlic|garlic]], pressed or grated
- 2 tsp [[Lemon|lemon zest]], finely grated
- 1 tbsp [[Lemon|lemon juice]]
- [[Lemon|Lemon]] wedges for serving
- ⅓ cup [[Parsley|parsley]], chopped

# Directions
1. With a racki nthe middle position, preheat the oven to 450°F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper or a nonstick baking mat and set aside.
2. Rinse each trout under cold running water and, using a paper towel, dry very well, inside and out. Sprinkle the cavity of each trout evenly with a ½ tsp of the Old Bay and ¼ tsp [[Black Pepper|black pepper]].
3. In a food processor, pulse the bread into crumbs the size of grains of rice, about 5 2-second pulses.
4. IN a medium skillet over medium heat, melt 2 tbsp of the butter. Add the breadcrumbs and ½ tsp salt and season with pepper. Cook, stirring constantly, until deeply browned and crisp, ±6 minutes. Scrape the toasted breadcrumbs into a medium bowl and set aside.
5. Wipe the skillet clean and return it to medium heat. Add the remaining butter and let warm until melted. Add the shallot, bell pepper, and ½ tsp salt and cook, stirring constantly, until the vegetables start to soften, ±2 minutes. Adjust the heat to low, cover, and cook until the vegetables appear moist, 1½ minutes longer. Add the garlic and lemon zest and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 40 seconds. Scrape the mixture into the bowl with the toasted breadcrumbs, add the lemon juice and parsley, and stir to combine into a stuffing. Adjust the seasoning with additional salt and pepper if necessary.
6. Place the trout on the prepared baking sheet. Spoon about ⅓ cup of the stuffing into the cavity of each trout, pressing it with your fingers. Arrange the fish so that there is about 1 inch between them.
7. Bake the trout until they feel firm when pressed gently with your giner, and the thickest part of the fish registers 140°F on an instant-read thermometer, ±12 minutes. Let rest for 5 minutes. Using a fish spatula, carefully transfer the trout to a serving platter and serve with lemon wedges.
